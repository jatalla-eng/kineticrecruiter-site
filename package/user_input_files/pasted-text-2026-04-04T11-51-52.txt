## PART 1: HOMEPAGE ISSUES TO FIX

The rendered homepage has several problems that need correcting. Here's every issue, in order of appearance.

---

### Issue 1: "Trusted by 500+ Recruitment Agencies" (hero top)
**Problem:** Fabricated social proof. The product hasn't launched. This is a credibility risk if anyone checks.
**Fix:** Remove entirely. Replace with nothing, or use a softer line like "Built for recruitment agencies" if space needs filling. Do not invent user numbers.

### Issue 2: "94% match accuracy" (hero bullet point)
**Problem:** Unsubstantiated metric. There's no data to support this claim. If a prospect asks "how do you measure that?" there's no answer.
**Fix:** Remove. Replace with "Transparent match scoring" or simply drop to three bullet points:
- 7-day free trial
- All AI features included
- No add-on fees

### Issue 3: Hero right-side image
**Problem:** The rendered image appears to be a stock photo of people ("Recruitment team collaborating"). The brand guide specifies no stock photography of people — illustrations only.
**Fix:** Replace with the illustrated pipeline graphic as specified in the Stitch prompt (candidate cards flowing through Sourcing → Interviewing → Offer stages with teal arrows). The AI Candidate Flow card mockup shown below the photo is good — that style of UI illustration should BE the hero image, not sit below a stock photo.

### Issue 4: Testimonials section (Emma Richardson, Marcus Chen, Sarah Thompson)
**Problem:** These are fabricated testimonials from people who don't exist at companies that don't exist. This is potentially illegal in Australia under the Australian Consumer Law (ACL) — misleading or deceptive conduct. It also destroys trust if anyone Googles "Talent Partners Australia" or "TechHire Solutions" and finds nothing.
**Fix:** Remove the entire testimonials section. Replace with nothing for now. Add real testimonials only when you have real users who agree to be quoted.

### Issue 5: Stats bar (500+ Agencies, 50,000+ Candidates Placed, 40% Faster Placements, 4.9/5 Customer Rating)
**Problem:** All fabricated. Same legal and credibility risk as testimonials.
**Fix:** Remove entirely. If you want a stats section post-launch, use real numbers. Until then, skip it.

### Issue 6: CTA section near bottom — "No credit card required"
**Problem:** Per your instruction, the 7-day trial should require a credit card. If they don't cancel in time, they get charged.
**Fix:** Remove "No credit card required" from every CTA on the site. The CTA text should simply be "Start Free Trial" with subtext "7-day free trial. Full access to every feature." No mention of credit card either way.

### Issue 7: FAQ — "How does the free trial work?" answer
**Problem:** If the FAQ says "no credit card required" it needs updating to match the new trial policy.
**Fix:** Update the answer to: "You get 7 days with 3 seats and full access to every feature on the Professional plan. Enter a payment method when you sign up, and if you don't cancel before the trial ends, your subscription begins automatically. You can cancel anytime during the trial at no charge."

### Issue 8: No pricing section on the homepage
**Problem:** The homepage doesn't include any pricing information. Visitors have to click through to a separate page. For a self-serve SaaS product, pricing visibility on the homepage reduces friction.
**Fix:** Add a pricing preview section above the CTA section. Show three tier cards with plan name, price, and 3-4 key differentiators. "See full details" link to the pricing page. Don't duplicate the full pricing page — just a teaser.

### Issue 9: Comparison tables — needs the "No Hidden Costs" strip
**Problem:** The dark background "$0 / $0 / $0" strip is present but sitting below the comparison tables. It should feel more visually integrated.
**Fix:** Keep as-is, but ensure the three cards clearly read:
- "$0 Implementation fee" / "Bullhorn charges $1K-$15K+"
- "$0 Training costs" / "Greenhouse charges $2K-$10K"
- "$0 AI add-on fees" / "Bullhorn charges $39+/mo per add-on"

### Issue 10: Footer "Created by MiniMax Agent"
**Problem:** Build tool attribution in the footer.
**Fix:** Remove.

### Issue 11: Missing pages
The navigation links to Features, Solutions, Pricing, and Resources pages that don't exist yet. These need to be built with the content below.

### Pricing FAQ

**Can I change plans at any time?**
Yes. Upgrade or downgrade at any time. Upgrades take effect immediately. Downgrades apply at the end of your current billing period.

**What happens when I hit my candidate limit?**
You'll see a notification as you approach your limit. Once you reach it, you won't be able to add new candidates until you upgrade. Your existing data is never affected.

**What happens when the trial ends?**
If you don't cancel during the 7-day trial, your subscription begins automatically on the plan you selected. You can cancel anytime during the trial at no charge.

**Is there a setup or implementation fee?**
No. There are no setup fees, implementation fees, or training costs. You sign up and start using the platform immediately. Agency plan includes a dedicated onboarding session.

**Do I need to pay extra for AI features?**
No. Every AI feature (resume parsing, candidate matching, career highlights, submission emails, natural language search) is included in every plan.

**How does seat-based billing work?**
You pay per active user. Add or remove seats at any time. Owners and Admins manage seats from the billing dashboard. Deactivated users don't count toward your seat total.

**Do you offer annual billing?**
Yes. Annual billing saves approximately 17% compared to monthly. All plans are available on both cycles.

**What payment methods do you accept?**
All major credit cards via Stripe. Invoicing is available for Agency plan customers with 10 or more seats.

**Is there a minimum contract?**
No. Monthly plans have no minimum commitment. Annual plans are billed upfront for 12 months.

## PART 5: FEATURE PAGES

### PAGE: AI Candidate Intelligence

**URL:** /features/ai-candidate-intelligence

**Hero headline:** The AI that finds, scores, and explains.

**Hero subtext:** Search your database in plain English. Get ranked results with match scores, factor breakdowns, and written reasons. Generate client-ready career summaries in one click.

#### Section 1: Natural Language Discovery

**Headline:** Stop writing Boolean. Start describing what you need.

Type what you're looking for the way you'd describe it to a colleague: "Full-stack developers with React and Node experience, at least 4 years, based in Sydney or open to remote." KineticRecruiter's semantic search engine understands the meaning behind your words, not just the keywords. It returns candidates ranked by relevance, with scores that reflect how well they actually fit.

This isn't keyword matching with a fancy interface. The platform builds semantic embeddings for every candidate profile, capturing the full context of their experience, skills, and career trajectory. When you search, it compares meaning, not strings. A candidate whose resume says "frontend engineering" and "server-side JavaScript" will surface for a "full-stack developer" search, even though the exact phrase never appears.

#### Section 2: Explainable Match Scoring

**Headline:** A number means nothing without the reasoning behind it.

Every candidate matched to a job receives a score from 0 to 100. But a score alone doesn't help you make decisions or explain them to clients. KineticRecruiter breaks down every score into contributing factors, shows how each factor was weighted, and provides a plain-English explanation of why this candidate was surfaced.

When a client asks "why did you put this person forward?", the answer is already written.

Key callouts:
- Factor-by-factor breakdown (skills match, experience level, location, industry alignment)
- Weight visibility so you understand what drove the score
- Written match reasons ready for client communication
- Suggested candidates ranked by score for every open role

#### Section 3: AI Career Highlights

**Headline:** Candidate summaries in seconds, not minutes.

Every recruiter knows the drill: open the resume, read through the entire work history, pick out the most relevant achievements, rewrite them as bullet points, paste into an email. For every single candidate. For every single submission.

KineticRecruiter generates career highlight bullet points automatically from the candidate's parsed resume. Action-driven. Context-aware. Ready to drop into a client submission email or review with a hiring manager.

No other ATS on the market generates client-ready career summaries.

### PAGE: Multi-Channel Candidate Intake

**URL:** /features/candidate-intake

**Hero headline:** Three ways in. Zero resumes lost.

**Hero subtext:** Upload one, drop fifty, or forward from your inbox. Every resume gets AI-parsed, deduplicated, and made searchable.

#### Section 1: Single and Bulk Upload

**Headline:** Drag, drop, done.

Upload a single resume or drop a batch of fifty. KineticRecruiter processes each file using Google Gemini AI to extract name, email, phone, location, skills, experience, education, work history, and LinkedIn URL into a structured candidate profile. PDF, DOC, and DOCX all supported.

If the AI parser is temporarily unavailable, a regex fallback parser still extracts core fields. No resume gets stuck in a queue or silently dropped. Progress tracking shows real-time status for every file in a bulk upload.

#### Section 2: Email Forwarding

**Headline:** Forward a resume. Get a candidate.

Receive a CV from a referral? Get an application notification from a job board? Forward the email to your KineticRecruiter inbox. The system extracts the attachment, validates the file type, runs AI parsing, and creates or updates the candidate profile.

If the candidate already exists (matched by email or name), their record gets updated with any new information without creating a duplicate. If they're new, a full profile is created, their LinkedIn photo is fetched, and a semantic embedding is generated so they're immediately searchable.

#### Section 3: LinkedIn Chrome Extension

**Headline:** See a profile. Import it.

The KineticRecruiter Chrome Extension sits on any LinkedIn profile page. Click import, and the candidate's name, photo, headline, work history, skills, and education are pulled into your ATS via API. One click. No PDF downloads. No manual entry.

#### Section 4: Smart Deduplication

**Headline:** One candidate. One record. Always.

Every intake channel checks for duplicates before creating a new record. Primary match is by email. Secondary match is by name. If a match is found, existing empty fields get updated with new data, and the resume file is replaced with the latest version.
