# MiniMax Agent — Final Design Review & Fixes

Based on review of all current pages as of 5 April 2026.

---

## FIXES THAT APPLY TO EVERY PAGE

### 1. Footer Logo
**Problem:** The KineticRecruiter logo in the footer is tiny and barely visible. It looks like a broken image or placeholder.
**Fix:** Increase the footer logo to at least 150px wide. It should be clearly readable — the full K icon + "KineticRecruiter" wordmark. Match the size and presence of the logo in the navbar.

### 2. "Created by MiniMax Agent"
**Problem:** Still appearing in the bottom-right corner of every page footer.
**Fix:** Remove from every page. This has been flagged in every previous review. Search the codebase for "MiniMax" and delete every instance.

### 3. Login Link
**Problem:** The "Login" link in the navbar currently goes nowhere or to a placeholder.
**Fix:** Set the Login link to point to `https://app.kineticrecruiter.com`. This is the production app URL. It should open in the same tab (not a new tab).

### 4. Footer Links — API Documentation
**Problem:** "API Documentation" is listed in the footer under Product. There's no API docs page ready for launch.
**Fix:** Remove "API Documentation" from the footer for now. It can be added back when the docs are ready. Replace with "Help Centre" as a placeholder, or just remove the link entirely.

## HOMEPAGE

### What's Working Well
- Hero section with the product UI mockup illustration looks good — the Kanban board with candidate cards and match score badges communicates the product clearly
- The alternating left-right layout for feature sections works
- Product illustrations (search results, score breakdown, career highlights, client CRM, Kanban, resume parsing) are well-executed and distinct from each other
- Comparison tables are clean and the KineticRecruiter column is properly highlighted
- The $0/$0/$0 dark strip is effective
- Pricing preview section with three tier cards is present and correct ($29/$59/$99)
- FAQ section is clean

### What Needs Fixing

**Issue 1: No human element on the homepage**
The homepage is the most visited page and it has zero photographs of people. Every section uses product illustrations, which is good for showing features, but the page feels cold and purely technical.

**Fix:** Add ONE photograph to the homepage. Place it in the "Candidate summaries that write themselves" section or the "Manage clients and candidates in one system" section. Replace the illustration in that ONE section with a candid workplace photograph — a recruiter at a laptop, or two people reviewing a screen together. This breaks up the illustration pattern and adds warmth without overdoing it.

Keep all other sections as product illustrations. One photo is enough for the homepage.

**Issue 2: Hero area could use a subtle human touch**
The product mockup in the hero is strong, but the overall hero feels purely digital.

**Fix:** Add a small circular photograph (approximately 60px diameter) in the bottom-left area of the hero section, partially overlapping the edge of the content area. Show a smiling recruiter or professional. This tiny human element anchors the hero without competing with the product mockup. It's subtle but effective.

## BLOG INDEX PAGE

### What's Working Well
- "Blog & Resources" headline with subtext
- Category filter tabs (All, Comparisons, AI in Recruitment, Agency Growth)
- Two blog post cards with titles, excerpts, dates, category tags, and "Read more" links
- Email subscription section at the bottom — good addition
- Reading time estimates shown (12 min, 8 min) — good

### What Needs Fixing

**Issue 1: Blog card images are placeholder icons**
Both blog cards show a generic teal icon on a light teal background instead of a real featured image. These look like broken placeholders.

**Fix:** Create proper featured images for each blog post. These should be branded OG-style images:

**Blog post 1 (ATS Comparison):** Teal background, white text reading "Best ATS for Recruitment Agencies" with the KineticRecruiter logo in the corner. Or a simple graphic showing 4 platform logos/names in a grid comparison layout.

**Blog post 2 (AI in Recruitment):** Teal background, white text reading "How AI Is Changing Recruitment in 2026" with a simple AI/sparkle icon. Or a workplace photograph with a teal colour overlay.

These images should be approximately 1200x630px (standard OG image ratio) and also serve as the Open Graph images for social sharing.

**Issue 2: No "Read more" preview on hover**
The cards are static. Adding a subtle hover effect (slight shadow increase, card lifts 2-3px) would improve interactivity.

## SOLUTIONS PAGES — PHOTO BACKGROUND APPROACH

The dark teal overlay on real photographs for the Solutions page heroes is working well. This should be the consistent pattern for all Solutions-type pages. It creates a clear visual hierarchy:

- **Feature pages** = product illustrations/mockups in heroes (clean, technical)
- **Solutions pages** = real photographs with teal overlay in heroes (human, warm)
- **Homepage** = product mockup (the flagship visual)
- **Pricing** = no hero image (data-focused)
- **Blog** = branded text banners

This differentiation is good. Keep it.
